Physical therapy is a necessity for those who rely on their bodies for work. Dancers specifically need to be in their best shape at all times. See how physical therapy helps maintain their bodies to be performance ready week after week.

Good flexibility allows the joints to improve their range of motion. For example, flexibility in the shoulder musculature allows a swimmer to ‘glide’ the arm through the water using shoulder elevation. This allows the joints to easily accommodate the desired joint angles without undue stress on the tissues around them. It therefore is essential for injury prevention.

State-of-the-art facilities and techniques used by Premier League footballers ensure that Royal Ballet dancers have long and successful careers. Full facilities and supplies are present to help support dancers 'training, injury prevention, and injury recovery.

Dancer’s hip, also known as snapping hip, is a condition characterized by a snapping sensation caused by motion of muscles and tendons over bony structures around the hip joint. It is often seen in young athletes such as dancers.
